A record of albums, films, TV shows, and artists I’ve been into recently. Click an item to read more.

01 Choke Enough (Deluxe) ALBUM
choke enough (Deluxe) by Oklou is one of my favorite albums right now. She just sounds so light and soft like she is floating over the music. The album explores themes of self discovery emotional vulnerability, and listening to it is an entirely immersive experience.
02 This Is Happening ALBUM
James Murphy pairs devastating lyrics with upbeat, almost celebratory arrangements. Each song layers chaotic and contrasting sounds that compliment each other perfectly. It's clever, sad, danceable, and comforting.
03 The Bends ALBUM
Just an extremely well done album. No skips, and has some of my favorite Radiohead songs of all time. I have been listening to this album a lot in the car recently, it just sounds so good, and is even better when you know the lyrics.
04 Punisher ALBUM
Front to back, Punisher is a masterpiece. Its a great time capsule of 2020. Every few months I hyperfixate on a song off of this album and learn to appreciate her artistry even more.
05 Ivan Pokidyshev ARTIST
Uses thermography to take photos of people then paint them into these glowing figures. Explores themes of science, life, love, and religion. Checkout his instragram here.
06 Amy Renee Webb ARTIST
Her glass looking religious iconography is unreal. Also, she shares my love for neon green. Checkout her instragram here.
07 Laura Footes ARTIST
Really inspiring painter for me. I love her figures so much. Checkout her instragram here.
08 Dylan Rheingold ARTIST
My friend Santi worked with her, super cool and fun paintings. Checkout her website here.
09 Call Me by Your Name film
Everything is so visually beautiful. I love the all of the shots and the lighting and the scenery. Timothée Chalamet was the perfect cast for Elio, he perfectly captures teenage embarrassment and emotion. The mix of languages and cultures is also done really well.
10 Love Lies Bleeding film
My favorite shot is the egg yolks and cigarettes going into the trash. Kristen Stewart and Katy O'Brian play off of each other fabulously. At times, the timeline can be a little confusing, but you realize by the end that they are not very concerned with delivering a realistic storyline.
11 Parasite film
Such a great story with amazing character development. This film makes me anxious and uncomfortable in the best way. It feels like I'm always bracing for something bad to happen, and then it hits out of nowhere. The characters are so interesting, with traits that are both relatable and frustrating. Visually, it's beautiful too.
12 Gone Girl film
Such a good thriller. I love the way the story is told. Rosamund Pike is such an incredible lead, all of her monologues are iconic.
13 Money Heist TV
The first few seasons are so well done. Each character is developed so carefully, and the plot is intricate and satisfying, even when things go wrong. It feels like a constant game of ping pong.
14 Schitt's Creek TV
Schitt's creek is probably the funniest show I've ever watched, and it is comedic in such an intelligent way. I actually laugh out loud so often. I was surprised by how visually pleasing it is too.
15 Sex and the City TV
So much more fabulous than people give it credit for. Everyone knows about the fashion, but every detail, down to what meal they are eating during what scene is perfect. I know Carrie gets a lot of hate but she is so funny and entertaining to me.
16 Hacks TV
Started watching this show after the Emmys. This is the kind of show you need to watch 3 times because the jokes are so fast and constant that you miss half of them the first time. I am so impressed by the writing and I often actually need to pause the show because I am in so much awe of how they even came up with the line that was just said. Great balance of comedy and plot.
