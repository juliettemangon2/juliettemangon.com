  • This Is Happening - LCD Soundsystem

    This is one of my favorite albums because of its musical and lyrical depth. Each track layers contrasting sounds in a way that somehow fits together perfectly. At first, the songs might seem simple or conversational, but repeated listens reveal clever wordplay, cultural references, and a lot of emotional weight. James Murphy pairs sad, sometimes devastating lyrics with music that's danceable and bright. That contrast makes it feel both personal and communal. It's experimental but still intentional.

Movies

  • Call Me by Your Name

    Everything is so visually beautiful. I love the way it was filmed and the locations they chose. Timothée Chalamet is incredible in this—he plays his role so well. I love the summer vibe and the mix of languages. He captures teenage embarrassment and emotion perfectly.

  • Love Lies Bleeding

    My favorite shot is the egg yolks and cigarettes going into the trash. I love this movie because it’s funny at times, sweet at others, but also completely wild. Both main actors are amazing. The timeline can be a little confusing, but I like how they play with it.

  • Parasite

    Such a great story with amazing character development. This movie makes me anxious and uncomfortable in the best way. It feels like I’m always bracing for something bad to happen, and then it hits out of nowhere. The characters are so interesting, with traits that are both relatable and frustrating. Visually, it’s beautiful too.

  • Gone Girl

    Such a good thriller. Creepy and strange in parts, but also really fun to watch. I love the way the story is told. Rosamund Pike is incredible—such a powerful lead.

TV Shows

  • Money Heist

    The first few seasons are so well done. Each character is developed so carefully, and the plot is intricate and satisfying, even when things go wrong. It feels like a constant game of ping pong. It’s too bad the show can only keep that level for so long.

  • Schitt's Creek

    Comedic genius. This show is funny and smart, and I actually laugh out loud so often. I was surprised by how visually pleasing it is too. Some moments are kind of gross, but that’s the point.

  • Girls

    Currently watching this and enjoying it. Super cringe, super relatable, and sometimes funny. Not much else to say.

  • Sex and the City

    So much more fabulous than people give it credit for. Everyone knows about the fashion, but even the little details are perfect. It’s exactly what I’d want this show to be. And yes, Carrie hating cooking is very relatable.
