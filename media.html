01 Choke Enough ALBUM
Choke Enough by Oklou is one of my favorite albums right now. She just sounds so light and soft like she is floating over the music. The album explores themes of self discovery emotional vulnerability, and listening to it is an entirely immersive experience.
02 This Is Happening ALBUM
James Murphy pairs devastating lyrics with upbeat, almost celebratory arrangements. Each song layers chaotic and contrasting sounds that compliment each other perfectly. It's clever, sad, danceable, and comforting.
03 The Bends ALBUM
Just an extremely well done album. Not one skip, and has some of my favorite Radiohead songs of all time. I have been listening to this album a lot in the car recently, it just sounds so good, and is even better when you know the lyrics.
04 Punisher ALBUM
Front to back, this album is a masterpiece. Punisher is exactly what we needed in 2020, a year full of fear, uncertainty, and isolation, which Phoebe Bridgers captures so well. She famously takes years to write songs, and it really shows in this album. Each song is clearly fully fledged out, there are is an orchestra when we want it, and simple acoustics when we don't.

09 Call Me by Your Name MOVIE
Everything is so visually beautiful. I love the all of the shots and the lighting and the scenery. Timothée Chalamet was the perfect cast for Elio, he perfectly captures teenage embarrassment and emotion. The mix of languages and cultures is also done really well.
10 Love Lies Bleeding MOVIE
My favorite shot is the egg yolks and cigarettes going into the trash. Kristen Stewart and Katy O'Brian play off of each other fabulously. At times, the timeline can be a little confusing, but you realize by the end that they are not very concerned with delivering a realistic storyline.
11 Parasite MOVIE
Such a great story with amazing character development. This movie makes me anxious and uncomfortable in the best way. It feels like I'm always bracing for something bad to happen, and then it hits out of nowhere. The characters are so interesting, with traits that are both relatable and frustrating. Visually, it's beautiful too.
12 Gone Girl MOVIE
Such a good thriller. Creepy and strange in parts, but also really fun to watch. I love the way the story is told. Rosamund Pike is such an incredible lead, all of her monologues are iconic.
13 Money Heist TV
The first few seasons are so well done. Each character is developed so carefully, and the plot is intricate and satisfying, even when things go wrong. It feels like a constant game of ping pong.
14 Schitt's Creek TV
Schitt's creek is probably the funniest show I've ever watched, and it is comedic in such an intelligent way. I actually laugh out loud so often. I was surprised by how visually pleasing it is too. Some moments are kind of gross, but that’s the point.
15 Girls TV
Currently watching this and enjoying it. Super cringe, super ridiculous, and sometimes funny and relatable. Not much else to say.
16 Sex and the City TV
So much more fabulous than people give it credit for. Everyone knows about the fashion, but every detail, down to what meal they are eating during what scene is perfect. It's exactly what I'd want this show to be. There are definitely some unlikable traits in the characters, I just find this show so entertaining that these supposed unlikable moments are just part of the fun.
