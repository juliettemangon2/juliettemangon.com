Depth

An exploration of nature, art, and technology beneath the surface.

Dive in

When I was 11, my dad introduced me to his life long passion for scubadiving. He immersed me into the underwater world, where I found comfort in the silence, and a sanctuary of beautifully intricate ecosystems to observe.
This project uses Gemini AI to combine his scubadiving photos with my water related paintings to imagine a world where humans, nature, and technology can coexist.

The camera captures what exists. The machine imagines what could.

200+ meters · The Twilight Zone

Where light dissolves
and meaning is made

Painting and photograph lose their borders here. What's left isn't natural or artificial. It's just new.

At this depth, the distinction between nature, human, and machine begins to dissolve. What remains is only pattern, light, and the persistent search for meaning in the dark.

These are not photographs. They are not paintings. They're something that neither my hand nor the algorithm could have made alone.

Artist Statement

About
the work

I'm Juliette Mangon, a computer scientist, mathematician, painter, and diver. I work where technology, creativity, and the natural world overlap.

This project started with my dad's diving photos. Images from a world I grew up in, full of silence and weird, beautiful light. I wanted to see what happens when that world meets a machine that's absorbed centuries of human art about the ocean.

What comes back isn't just a stylized image. It's something that feels like a shared memory — as if the machine, too, has dreamed of the deep.
